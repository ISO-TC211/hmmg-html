The "LBS_Routing" service is functionality that finds optimal or near optimal routes meeting requested criteria. The specifics of the type of request that can be made, the type of cost functions that can be used, the types of constraints and preferences that can be specified would all be part of the registered metadata of the routing service. <br/><br/>

Operation
Public
route( routeCriteria[1..*]: LBS_RouteCriteria,
Default:
Kind: inout
Stereotype:
The parameter "routeCriteria" contains criteria for each leg of the journey that will result in a set of continuous subroutes in the returned route. Each subroute will be the optimal or a near optimal route meeting the criteria for that section. The mechanisms for handling overall criteria or personal criteria is left to the implementation of the service.
):LBS_Route
Details:
sequential
Notes: The operation "route" in a routing service finds the optimal or a near optimal route meeting as much of the criteria passed to it as possible. The returned route must start and end where selected, and pass through the way points during its extent. It must meet the criteria, and as many of the preferences as practical. The route criteria may be passed as individual subroutes, each ending where the next begins. This is useful if a schedule or list of points need be reached in a particular order. The resultant route should meet the criteria for each leg or subroute of the journey. <br/>
