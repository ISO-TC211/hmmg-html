The NT_Turn object represents a mechanism for traversing from one link to another. Each turn will be located at a node of the underlying topology<br/>and will form the bridge for a link entering the node and a link exiting the node. The use of turns allows a restricted network (one with constraints) to be represented by an unrestricted one.<br/>The use of the word "turn" does not necessarily reflect a change of direction. Going straight through an intersection is one such viable option and would be represented by a "NT_Turn." In public transport<br/>such as a rail system<br/>the "NT_Turns" are routes through "stations" connecting one incoming "train" with one outgoing "train."  NT_Turns are thus called "connections" when speaking of airport<br/>and have temporal constraints based on the availability of the "links" that is<br/>on flight times.<br/><br/><br/>{fromLink.endNode = toLink.startNode = topology}<br/>(isTraversable} implies {fromLink.isTraversable}<br/>(isTraversable} implies {toLink.isTraversable<br/>
