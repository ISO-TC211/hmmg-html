A maneuver is a legal sequence of actions, given by a sequence of turns, each of which terminates on the link that is the start of the next turn. Most maneuvers are single turns, but some turn's traversability are dependent on route history (as a ending turn in a U-turn across a divided highway). Since in this case, the turn's traversability cannot be decided by a single link's history, the maneuver extends that history to a sufficient  length to determine traversability. The total length of most maneuvers is quite short.<br/>
